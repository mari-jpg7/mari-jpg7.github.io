Andres Calamaro

Andres Calamaro is an Argentinian music icon as his music contributed to the rise of popularity of Argentina's national rock genre. His first major accomplishment began in 1982, when he joined the band "Los Abuelos de la Nada" and composed the iconic Latin American song 'Mil Horas'. He was behind many of this band's lyrics until 1990, when he moved to Spain. There, he formed another remembered band named "Los Rodriguez" along with Ariel Rot and Julian Infante. Their biggest song recorded was "Sin Documentos" and is also considered an iconic song within the Hispanic community for its romantic lyrics. After the disbandment of Los Rodriguez in 1996, his career in solo took off with the album "Honestidad Brutal" (1999) and "Alta Suciedad" (1997) which were a major success with songs like "Flaca","Loco","Te quiero igual", and many more. This success make Andres Calamaro a loved artist in Argentina and in many other Latin American countries as he continues to tour and sell out stadiums in Argentina, Chile, Peru, Colombia, etc.
